# Product Requirements Document

## Product name

`Kairos`

## Product category

Guided spiritual discernment for important decisions.

Kairos is not being positioned as:

- a generic Christian chatbot
- a devotional content app
- a passive community product

Kairos is being positioned as a guided decision process.

## Product summary

Kairos helps a person move from ambiguity to a concrete, obedient, executable decision through a structured process that includes reflection, prayer, scriptural engagement, reduction of ambiguity, commitment, and follow-through.

## Executive brief

Kairos is being conceived as a decision-guidance product for believers who already value prayer, Scripture, and obedience but struggle to move from internal ambiguity to concrete action.

The project is based on a simple conviction:

people usually do not need more spiritual sources; they need a more disciplined process around the sources they already trust.

The first strategic objective is not scale.

It is to establish a clear, credible, repeatable discernment process that people can actually complete and leaders can confidently distribute.

## Background

The project emerges from a central observation:

many believers already pray, read Scripture, and seek counsel, but still struggle to close important decisions with clarity and obedience.

The issue is not primarily lack of spiritual source.

The issue is the absence of an ordered process that helps the person:

- define the actual decision
- distinguish emotion from conviction
- seek God intentionally about that decision
- recognize when clarity is present
- act on what has become clear

## Problem statement

Believers often face important life decisions with valid spiritual inputs available, but without a disciplined process to discern, confirm, and obey clearly under emotional pressure.

## Solution statement

Kairos introduces a guided discernment process that helps a person move through one decision at a time and arrive at a concrete next action that they believe is aligned with what God is showing them.

## Why now

Several conditions make this project timely:

- conversational interfaces are now natural and expected
- language models can support guided interaction and ambiguity reduction
- communities of faith are already comfortable with digital communication channels
- many people seek clarity privately before speaking to a pastor or leader

## Strategic opportunity

Kairos sits at the intersection of:

- spiritual life
- important personal decisions
- conversational interfaces
- low-friction digital distribution through communities

This creates the possibility of a product that is:

- personally relevant
- emotionally urgent
- spiritually serious
- commercially simple

Few products occupy this territory with structure and discipline.

## Target user

### Primary user

A believer with an active spiritual life who is currently facing an important decision and feels unable to close it clearly.

### Characteristics

- believes God speaks and guides
- values prayer and Scripture
- desires obedience
- feels tension, ambiguity, or repeated hesitation
- is willing to submit to a guided process

### Non-primary user

Kairos is not initially optimized for:

- people looking only for generic encouragement
- people without spiritual framework
- people seeking debate or theological novelty
- people who only want validation of what they already decided

## Core use case

The first canonical use case is:

deciding whether to continue or end a romantic relationship.

This use case is foundational because it concentrates:

- emotional load
- spiritual seriousness
- fear of loss
- repeated ambiguity
- high consequences

## Secondary future use cases

Once the core flow proves itself, adjacent use cases may include:

- job and calling transitions
- relocation decisions
- financial commitments
- conflict and reconciliation decisions
- leadership or ministry direction questions

These are future extensions, not current scope commitments.

## User job to be done

When I am stuck in an important decision before God, help me move from confusion and inner noise to a clear and executable decision without replacing prayer, Scripture, or the Holy Spirit.

## Product principles

### 1. One decision at a time

Each session exists to resolve one concrete decision.

### 2. Structure over drift

The system guides and converges. It does not wander freely.

### 3. God remains the source

The product supports discernment; it does not claim authority over revelation or conscience.

### 4. Participation is required

The user must think, pray, read, respond, choose, and act.

### 5. Closure matters

A session is incomplete if it ends only in insight and not in a decision or defined waiting step.

### 6. The process must feel spiritually serious

The experience must never feel like casual chat entertainment.

It must carry:

- gravity
- gentleness
- clarity
- reverence

## Experience flow

### Phase 1 — Define the decision

The user arrives with ambiguity.

Kairos listens, redirects, and proposes formulations until one real decision is defined.

### Phase 2 — Separate the internal noise

The user identifies feelings, desires, and fears so they stop operating invisibly.

### Phase 3 — Seek God intentionally

Kairos guides the user into Scripture and prayer with a specific lens and concrete prompts.

### Phase 4 — Collect and reduce ambiguity

Kairos helps the user articulate what they understood and what they perceived, then reduces vague language into actionable clarity.

### Phase 5 — Decide and commit

The user names the decision explicitly and defines the next concrete action and timing.

### Phase 6 — Execute and sustain

The user confirms execution, and the process protects against emotional reopening without genuinely new information.

## UX posture

Kairos should feel like:

- guided but not controlling
- spiritually serious but not heavy-handed
- clear but not mechanical
- warm but not vague

The core interaction pattern should always communicate:

“You are being accompanied through a meaningful decision.”

## Product requirements

### Functional requirements

1. The system must support a six-phase guided decision flow.
2. The system must maintain session memory across phases.
3. The system must support both freeform input and constrained option selection.
4. The system must be able to propose clearer decision formulations when the user remains ambiguous.
5. The system must support directed scriptural reflection prompts.
6. The system must support a pause and return pattern.
7. The system must end in either a concrete decision or a defined waiting path.
8. The system must support a paywall after phase 1.
9. The system must support code-based access for testing and distribution.

### Non-functional requirements

1. The tone must remain respectful, gentle, and clear.
2. The interaction must not feel like unconstrained open chat.
3. The experience must remain usable on mobile-first surfaces.
4. The system must avoid unnecessary cognitive overload.
5. The product must preserve a strong sense of seriousness and trust.

## Success metrics

Early success should be measured by:

- percentage of users who complete phase 1
- percentage of users who continue past the paywall or activate a code
- percentage of users who reach phase 5
- percentage of users who state a concrete decision
- percentage of users who return to confirm execution

## Validation milestones

### Milestone 1

The process can be understood and completed by real users without live facilitation.

### Milestone 2

Users report that the process helped them name and close a real decision.

### Milestone 3

Trusted leaders are willing to distribute access codes because the experience feels safe, helpful, and serious.

### Milestone 4

Some users return with a second decision, showing that Kairos is not only interesting but reusable.

Longer-term signals include:

- repeat sessions per user
- code-based community adoption
- leader willingness to distribute access

## Commercial model

Current business hypothesis:

- one decision session is the core unit of value
- the initial price point is low-friction and impulse-friendly
- multi-session packs increase lifetime value
- leaders and pastors can distribute access codes into communities

Current working pricing hypothesis:

- 1 session: `$9`
- 5 sessions: `$29`

## Risks

### Product risks

- the experience may feel too rigid
- the process may be abandoned at pauses
- the session may feel serious but not necessary

### Spiritual risks

- users may misread the product as spiritual authority
- biblical handling may feel shallow or imprecise if not carefully designed
- some decision types may require pastoral escalation beyond guided software

### Commercial risks

- price may feel too low and weaken perceived seriousness
- price may feel too high for first-use experimentation
- code usage may produce engagement without paid conversion

## Open decisions

The following remain intentionally open at this stage:

- Bible licensing and final scriptural source strategy
- level of LLM freedom versus constrained scriptural routing
- first-launch breadth across decision categories
- final tone calibration
- community and leader distribution model

## Immediate next step

The immediate next step is not full product build.

The immediate next step is to continue refining the documentation until the project has a stable strategic foundation for design, implementation, testing, and distribution.
