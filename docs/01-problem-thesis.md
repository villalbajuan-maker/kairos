# Problem Thesis

## Problem statement

People have access to valid spiritual sources of direction, but often lack a disciplined process that helps them seek intentionally, discern clearly, and close in obedience when a real decision is in front of them.

The issue is not the failure of prayer, Scripture, the Holy Spirit, or pastoral counsel.

The issue is the human process around discernment.

## Clarification of what does not fail

The project assumes the following:

- prayer does not fail
- Scripture does not fail
- godly counsel does not fail
- the Holy Spirit does not fail

Therefore, Kairos is not built on suspicion toward spiritual sources.

It is built on realism about human fragility in how those sources are received, processed, and obeyed.

## Where the problem really appears

The problem appears in moments like:

- ending or continuing a relationship
- staying in or leaving a job
- making a financial commitment
- making a move that affects one’s calling, family, or direction
- deciding how to respond to conflict, tension, or misalignment

These are not abstract spiritual questions.

They are concrete decisions with emotional pressure, spiritual weight, and real-world consequences.

## Core human frictions

The main frictions are:

- ambiguity about what the real decision is
- emotional noise mixed with conviction
- desire mixed with obedience
- fear mixed with prudence
- delay disguised as “waiting on God”
- repeated reopening of what was already clear
- inability to convert spiritual clarity into action

## Working thesis

People do not mainly fail because they lack direction.

They fail because they do not have a clear and disciplined process that helps them:

- define the decision truthfully
- seek God intentionally about that decision
- reduce ambiguity without self-deception
- obey the clarity they receive

## Why this matters

When the discernment process remains disordered, the result is often:

- repeated delay
- repeated cycles
- emotional exhaustion
- poor decisions
- false peace
- weakened confidence in one’s own ability to obey

Kairos treats this as a serious operational problem inside spiritual life.
