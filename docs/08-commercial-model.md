# Commercial Model

## Commercial principle

Kairos is not monetizing “faith as content.”

Kairos is monetizing a high-value process:

helping a person close an important decision with clarity and obedience.

## Unit of value

The natural commercial unit is one decision session.

The user is not primarily buying access to software.

The user is buying help for a decision that already carries weight, cost, and urgency.

## Current commercial thesis

The clearest initial model is:

- pay per decision
- upsell into multi-decision packs
- distribute free-access codes through trusted leaders and communities

## Why not start with subscription

A subscription model introduces pressure toward:

- passive usage
- lower seriousness
- artificial retention

Kairos is strongest when attached to a real decision event.

That makes per-decision pricing more aligned with the actual value exchange.

## Current pricing hypothesis

Initial working hypothesis:

- 1 decision session: `$9`
- 5 decision sessions: `$29`

These numbers are hypotheses, not final truths.

They are designed to balance:

- impulse accessibility
- seriousness of value
- room for repeat use

## Paywall placement

The current paywall thesis is:

- Phase 1 is free
- the paywall appears once the decision is clearly defined

This moment is strategically strong because:

- the user already experienced clarity
- the system already proved seriousness
- the core value has not yet been delivered in full

## Role of access codes

Codes are not only discounts.

They are activation mechanisms.

Their initial strategic use is:

- pastors and leaders distributing access
- controlled testing with communities
- reducing friction for first exposure
- generating learning volume without weakening perceived value

## Long-term commercial possibilities

Possible future commercial layers may include:

- decision packs
- church or ministry distribution programs
- leader dashboards
- premium facilitated journeys

But those should remain secondary until the core session proves real demand and repeat use.
