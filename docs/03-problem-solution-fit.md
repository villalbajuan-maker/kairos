# Problem–Solution Fit

## Problem

People have access to spiritual direction but often lack a disciplined process to seek clearly, discern honestly, and obey concretely when a real decision is in front of them.

## Solution

Kairos guides a person through a structured discernment process that:

- defines the real decision
- separates emotion from conviction
- activates Scripture and prayer intentionally
- reduces ambiguity
- closes in action

## Why the fit is strong

The fit is strong because the solution maps directly to the frictions inside the problem.

| Problem friction | Kairos response |
|---|---|
| Vague problem statements | Forces definition of one concrete decision |
| Emotional noise | Separates feelings, desires, and fears |
| Unstructured spiritual search | Activates directed Scripture and prayer |
| Persistent ambiguity | Uses guided reduction toward concrete language |
| Self-deception | Introduces confrontation and confirmation |
| Delay and reopening | Requires explicit closure and action |

## Central assumption behind the fit

If a person is guided through an ordered discernment process, the likelihood increases that they will:

- recognize what is actually clear
- stop confusing resistance with confusion
- act on what they believe God is showing them

If that assumption is true, Kairos has real problem–solution fit.

## Where the fit is strongest

The fit is strongest for:

- believers with an active spiritual framework
- people facing an important and current decision
- people who already want to obey
- people who feel stuck between clarity and action

## Where the fit is weak

The fit is weak for:

- people who do not want truth, only validation
- people without urgency
- people looking only for content or inspiration
- people who reject process and discipline

## Current conclusion

Kairos appears to fit a real and intense problem:

not lack of spiritual source, but lack of disciplined discernment leading to obedience.
